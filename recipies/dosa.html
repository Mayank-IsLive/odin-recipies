Masala Dosa With Chatni At Home

Discriptions :

Masala dosa is a popular South Indian , featuring a crispy, thin crepe made from fermented rice and lentil batter, typically filled with a spiced potato mixture. It's served with sambar (a lentil-based vegetable stew) and chutneys (often coconut and tomato-based). The spiced potato filling, or "masala," usually includes boiled and mashed potatoes, onions, and various spices. Variations exist, such as Mysore masala dosa, which includes a layer of red chutney, and rava masala dosa, made with semolina.

Ingredient :

Dosa Batter Ingredient :

  • Rice: Usually parboiled or regular rice, sometimes with the addition of idli rice (a type of short-grain rice)
  • Urad Dal (Black Lentils): Provides protein and helps with fermentation.
  • Fenugreek Seeds (Methi): Adds flavor and helps with crispness.
  • Salt: To Taste.
  • Water: For soaking and grinding.

Potato Masala Filling Ingredients:

  • Potatoes: Boiled, peeled, and mashed or cubed.
  • Onions: Finely chopped.
  • Green Chilies:Chopped (optional, for spice).
  • Mustard Seeds: Used for tempering.
  • Chana Dal (Split Chickpeas): Adds a nutty flavor and texture.
  • Curry Leaves: For aroma and flavor.
  • Turmeric Powder:Adds color and flavor.
  • Salt: To taste.
  • Oil or Ghee: For cooking the filling.
  • Cilantro (Coriander Leaves):For garnish.
  • Ginger: Chopped or grated.

Optional/Variation:

  • Coconut: Grated coconut can be added to the filling or served as a chutney.
  • Sambar: A lentil-based vegetable stew is a common accompaniment.
  • Chutneys: Various chutneys like coconut chutney, tomato chutney, or red chutney are served with the dosa.
  • Other Vegetables: Carrots, peas, or other vegetables can be added to the potato filling.
  • Dried Red Chilies: Used for tempering or in chutneys.
  • Asofoetida (Hing):Adds a unique flavor.

How To Make Masala Dosa Batter:

  1. Soak rice and urad dal separately for 4-6 hours.
  2. Grind the soaked rice and urad dal (with fenugreek seeds if using) into a smooth batter, adding water gradually.
  3. Add salt and mix well.
  4. Ferment the batter overnight (or for at least 8 hours).
  5. Adjust consistency with water before making dosas.

How To Make Potato Masala Filling:

  1. Heat oil/ghee in a pan and add mustard seeds.
  2. Add chana dal and curry leaves, and saute until golden.
  3. Add onions, green chilies, and ginger, and saute until onions are translucent.
  4. Add turmeric powder and salt, and mix well.
  5. Add mashed potatoes and mix thoroughly, ensuring all ingredients are well combined.
  6. Garnish with coriander leaves.

Chutneys And Sambar (optional but traditional):

  1. Chutneys: Coconut chutney, tomato chutney, or red garlic chutney are common accompaniments.
  2. Sambar: A lentil-based vegetable stew, often served alongside the dosa.

Making The Dosas:

  1. Heat a griddle or tava (flat pan).
  2. Pour a ladle of dosa batter onto the hot surface and spread it into a thin, circular crepe.
  3. Drizzle oil or ghee around the edges.
  4. Cook until the edges start to lift and the dosa is golden brown.
  5. Spread the potato masala on one half of the dosa.
  6. Fold the dosa in half or into a roll.
  7. Serve hot with your choice of chutneys and sambar.
